## Design Context

### Users

**Primary audiences (equal weight):**
- **Community organisers / web admins** (power users): Maintain listings, curate categories, manage team members for a local Resilience Web. They return frequently, need efficient workflows, and care about how their community is represented.
- **Residents / visitors**: Discover local groups working on social and environmental justice. They arrive curious, browse by category or map, and leave with a connection to act on.

**Usage context**: Both audiences are civic-minded people looking for real-world connection. They're often on personal devices, sometimes mobile, browsing with intent but not urgency. The interface should reward exploration without demanding expertise.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: Activist, urgent, hopeful.

**Voice**: Direct and earnest, never corporate or polished-smooth. The tone says "people are already doing this work -- here's how to find them and join in." It should feel like a well-organized community noticeboard, not a SaaS product or government directory.

**Emotional goals**: Visitors should feel *inspired to act* and *curious to explore*. The interface should convey momentum -- that things are happening, people are connected, and there's a place for you in it.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Grounded, warm, textured. Think community print shop, not venture-backed startup. The design should feel handmade-but-organized -- like a zine that happens to have great information architecture.

**What it is NOT**:
- Not corporate/NGO (no stock imagery vibes, no sterile white dashboards)
- Not tech startup (no gradient CTAs, no "get started free" energy)
- Not government directory (no bureaucratic grids, no lifeless typography)

**Color direction**: The forest green identity is strong and worth keeping as a foundation -- it signals environment and action. Open to evolving the palette to be more distinctive: richer tints, earthier neutrals, bolder accent moments.

**Typography direction**: Open to moving beyond Poppins to something with more character. The right typeface should feel approachable but have an edge -- not a geometric sans that could belong to any brand.

**Theme**: Light mode primary. Dark mode variables exist but not active; not a current priority. Design for light mode first; dark mode can follow later.

### Design Principles

1. **Momentum over polish**: The interface should feel alive and active -- like a movement in progress, not a finished product on a shelf. Favour energy and warmth over sleekness.

2. **Explore, don't funnel**: Resist the SaaS impulse to funnel users toward conversion. People should wander, discover connections they didn't expect, and feel the density of community activity.

3. **Grounded, not generic**: Every visual choice should feel like it belongs to *this* project -- the environmental movement, place-based action, grassroots organising. No interchangeable tech-product aesthetics.

4. **Organised clarity**: Activist energy doesn't mean chaos. Information should be easy to scan, categories clear, and actions obvious. The best community noticeboards are the ones someone took time to organise well.

5. **Accessible by default**: General WCAG best practice throughout. The audience is broad -- age, ability, device, and digital confidence all vary. Don't optimise for power users at the expense of newcomers.

### Technical Constraints

- **Framework**: Next.js 16 with Tailwind CSS 4.2
- **Component system**: shadcn/ui (New York style) with Radix UI primitives
- **Current font**: Poppins (400, 600) via @fontsource -- can be changed
- **Current palette**: Forest green primary (`#09622f`, `#2B8257`, `#3A8159`) with HSL CSS variables
- **Icons**: Lucide React primary, react-icons secondary
- **Maps**: Leaflet with marker clustering
- **Network graph**: vis-network for relationship visualization
